Palak Paneer

Palak Paneer is a North Indian disk known for its vibrant green color and rich, Creamy texture. it is a vegetarian delicacy that combines fresh spinch (Palak) with indian cottage cheese (Paneer), simmered in a flavorful blend of spices.

Origins

Palak Paneer traces its roots to the Indian subcontinent, where it is believed to have originated in the Punjab region. This region is known for its fertile lands and abundance of fresh produce, including spinach and dairy products like paneer. Over time, Palak Paneer has become a staple in Indian cuisine and is enjoyed not only in India but also worldwide.

Key Ingredients

The two primary ingredients in Palak Paneer are spinach and paneer:
  • Spinach (Palak):Fresh spinach leaves are the star ingredient of this dish. They are blanched and pureed to create the vibrant green base of the curry.
  • Paneer : Paneer is a fresh Indian cheese made by curdling milk with a coagulant like lemon juice or vinegar. It has a mild, creamy flavor and a firm texture that holds up well in curries.

Health Benfits

Aside from its delicious taste, Palak Paneer offers several health benefits:
  • Nutrient-Rich: Spinach is packed with vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants, making Palak Paneer a nutritious choice.
  • Good Source of Protein: Paneer provides a good amount of protein, making it a satisfying vegetarian option.
  • Low in Calories: When prepared without excessive oil or cream, Palak Paneer can be a relatively low-calorie dish, suitable for those watching their calorie intake.

How to make palak Paneer

Ingredients:

  • 250 grams paneer (Indian cottage cheese), cut into cubes
  • 500 grams fresh spinach leaves (palak)
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, finely chopped
  • 2 green chilies, chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-inch ginger, grated
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons oil or ghee
  • 2 tablespoons cream (optional)
  • Fresh cilantro leaves for garnish

Instructions :

  1. Preparation:

    • Wash the spinach leaves thoroughly under running water to remove any dirt or impurities. Drain and set aside.
    • Blanch the spinach: Bring a pot of water to a boil. Add the spinach leaves and blanch for 2-3 minutes until wilted. Immediately transfer the spinach to a bowl of ice water to stop the cooking process. Drain and set aside.
    • Puree the blanched spinach in a blender or food processor until smooth. Set aside.

  2. Cooking:

      Heat oil or ghee in a pan over medium heat. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
      Add chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
      Add minced garlic, grated ginger, and chopped green chilies. Sauté for a minute until fragrant.
      Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they turn soft and mushy.
      Stir in coriander powder, turmeric powder, and salt. Mix well and cook for another minute.
      Add the spinach puree to the pan and mix well with the onion-tomato mixture. Cook for 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the spinach is heated through.
      Add the paneer cubes to the spinach mixture and gently stir to combine. Cook for 2-3 minutes until the paneer is heated through.
      Sprinkle garam masala over the Palak Paneer and mix well. Adjust salt and spices according to taste.
      If using cream, stir it into the Palak Paneer just before serving.

  3. serving

    • Transfer the Palak Paneer to a serving dish.
    • Garnish with fresh cilantro leaves.
    • Serve hot with naan, roti, or rice.
