Gajar Ka Halwa (Carrot Halwa)

Gajar Halwa, also known as Carrot Halwa, is a traditional Indian dessert made with grated carrots, milk, sugar, and ghee. It is flavored with cardamom and garnished with nuts, making it a rich and indulgent treat enjoyed during festive occasions and celebrations.

Ingredients:

  • 500 grams carrots, grated
  • 500 ml full-fat milk
  • 1/2 cup sugar (adjust according to taste)
  • 4 tablespoons ghee (clarified butter)
  • 1/4 teaspoon cardamom powder
  • A handful of chopped nuts (cashews, almonds, pistachios) for garnish
  • Raisins (optional)

Instructions

  1. Preparation:

    • Wash, peel, and grate the carrots.
    • Heat the milk in a heavy-bottomed pan and bring it to a boil. Reduce the heat to low and let it simmer while you prepare the carrots.

  2. Cooking Steps:

    1. Heat ghee in a separate pan or kadhai over medium heat.
    2. Add the grated carrots and saut√© for 5-7 minutes until they soften and release their moisture.
    3. Pour the simmering milk over the carrots and stir well. Cook on low heat, stirring occasionally, until the carrots are cooked and the milk reduces to half its original quantity.
    4. Add sugar and cardamom powder to the carrot-milk mixture. Stir well and continue cooking until the sugar dissolves and the mixture thickens to a pudding-like consistency.
    5. Add chopped nuts and raisins (if using) and mix well. Cook for another 2-3 minutes.
    6. Finally, add a tablespoon of ghee and mix well. This enhances the flavor and gives the Gajar Halwa a rich aroma.

  3. serving:

    • Serve hot or cold, garnished with additional chopped nuts. Gajar Halwa can be enjoyed on its own or with a scoop of vanilla ice cream for an extra indulgent treat.

Tips:

  • Use fresh, tender carrots for the best flavor and texture.
  • Adjust the amount of sugar according to your preference for sweetness.
  • For a vegan version, you can substitute ghee with coconut oil or vegan butter.
  • Leftover Gajar Halwa can be refrigerated and reheated before serving.

About the Dish:

Gajar Halwa holds a special place in Indian cuisine and is often prepared during festivals like Diwali and Holi, as well as during winter months when carrots are in season. Its rich, creamy texture and aromatic flavor make it a beloved dessert that is enjoyed by people of all ages.
